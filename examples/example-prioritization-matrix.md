# Example AI Initiative Prioritization Matrix

Organizations often identify multiple AI opportunities. A prioritization matrix helps leaders select initiatives based on business value and implementation readiness.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

AI initiatives may be evaluated across several dimensions:

- Business Impact
- Data Readiness
- Implementation Complexity
- Organizational Readiness
- Compliance and Risk Considerations

Each dimension can be scored on a scale (for example 1–5).

---

| Use Case | Business Impact | Data Readiness | Implementation Complexity | Organizational Readiness | Risk Level | Priority Score |
|----------|----------------|---------------|---------------------------|--------------------------|-----------|---------------|

| Sales Forecasting | 5 | 3 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 18 |
| Document Automation | 3 | 4 | 2 | 4 | 2 | 15 |

---

## Prioritization Categories

Based on scoring, initiatives may be categorized as:

**Pursue Immediately**  
High value and strong readiness.

**Prepare for Future Implementation**  
Promising initiatives that require additional data or preparation.

**Monitor**  
Ideas worth revisiting later as organizational readiness improves.

**Do Not Pursue**  
Initiatives that currently offer limited value or present high risk.
