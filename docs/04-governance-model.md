# Governance Model for AI Initiatives

This section defines the governance structure required to oversee AI initiatives, clarify decision authority, and ensure responsible delivery across the organization.

AI initiatives introduce new considerations around risk, ethics, compliance, and operational impact. A governance structure helps ensure that initiatives are evaluated responsibly and delivered effectively.

## Key Governance Roles

### Executive Sponsor
Provides strategic direction and ensures alignment with organizational priorities.

### Steering Committee
Reviews major initiatives, evaluates risks, and approves investments.

### Program Lead
Coordinates delivery across teams and maintains alignment with governance processes.

### Technical Lead
Oversees architecture, data requirements, and model implementation.

### Business Owner
Responsible for operational outcomes and adoption of the AI solution.

### Risk and Compliance Representatives
Evaluate legal, privacy, and regulatory considerations.

## Governance Cadence

Typical governance activities may include:

- monthly steering committee reviews
- risk and compliance assessments
- executive reporting on progress and outcomes
- decision logs for major program milestones

A clear governance structure ensures AI initiatives are delivered with transparency and accountability.
